Mental Health Resources

Self-Care Simplified offers small actions to help in the moment. If you'd like to explore deeper information or additional support, the following organizations provide trusted mental health resources.

SAMHSA National Helpline

Treatment Referral and information service for mental health and substance use.

NAMI - National Alliance on Mental Illness

Education, support groups, and mental health information

Feeling Overwhelmed

Explains how stress and overwhelm affect the body and provides simple strategies to calm the nervous system

Stress Overload – Cleveland Clinic

Overview of stress, its effects on mental health, and practical ways to manage it.

American Psychological Association - Stress Management

Research-based but practical articles on stress, resilience, and emotional well-being.

Greater Good Science Center – Managing Stress

Feeling Anxious

Simple grounding exercises that help calm racing thoughts.

Cleveland Clinic - Grounding techniques

Reliable information about anxiety and treatment options

National Institute of Mental Health - Anxiety Disorders

Feeling Angry

Strategies for understanding and managing anger in healthy ways.

American Psychological Association - Anger Control

Explains what causes anger and how to manage intense emotions

Mayo Clinic – Anger Management

Feeling Sadness

Practice tools and worksheets based on behavioral activation

Centre for Clinical Interventions – Managing Low Mood

Accessible explanations and self-help strategies.

Mental Health America – Depression

Positive Psychology Principles

Tools and ideas for building positive emotional habits.

Crisis Support

Call or text 988 for free 24/7 emotional support in the U.S.

>988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline

Text HOME to 741741 to connect with a trained crisis counselor.
